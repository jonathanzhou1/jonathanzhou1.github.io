The Life of 530 Words

A collection of poetry

Scholastics Arts and Writing award winning poem - 2017
The Life of 530 Words


Life. Such a fickle thing. So fragile, and yet, once taken away, it can never be returned.

Brothers, a bond through birth.

Brother,
You always said that you were the older brother,
And I was the younger.
You wanted to shield me from the “wind and the rain.”
We would be together through the toughest of times,
And as one, search for meaning in life.
I have you, my older brother, forever in my heart.
I also wanted to help you,
But I feared the storm.
Sunshine in public, and spiteful in private.
Why could I never tell you the feelings that I felt?

Brother, when I was young, I always asked you,
“Why were you always the older and I, younger?”
I waited.
I was never the good one,
Why did you never give up on me?

Brother,
I saw you in the rain and wind, facing the storm for me.
It was harsh,
But why did you never shed a tear?
I knew you bled on the inside.

Brother,
I want to stand together with you,
I know that you are still human.
I have you, my older brother, in my heart,
And it is my turn to shield you.
I never had the time to tell you the words I wanted to say,
The bonds of brothers.
The hearts of brothers.
The love of brothers.

Love. The most formidable power in the universe.

Beloved,
I’ve been lonely for so long,
And it’s still not better.
It feels like the whole world is scorning me.
I was weak.
You always handled it so easily.
I think I can feel the taste of the tears now.

Beloved,
Clearly, you loved me very much.
I have forgotten how it felt.
There are still haunting dreams in the long night.
I better not think about you.
When the time comes,
They’ll know how much it hurts.

Beloved,
The happiness then was so captivating.
And I just let it go.
The dreams that felt like they were coming true…
In a blink of the eye, they were all broken.
I hope that you are doing well.
I’m sorry.
I guess I was sentenced forever alone.
Unescapable.
You are unforgettable.

Children. The legacy of the past and the bridge to the future.

Son,
By the time you read this,
I’ll be gone back into the earth that borne me.
There were many mistakes I made in my life,
Make sure you do not follow my footsteps.
Society may be harsh,
But promise me that you’ll never let your loved one go.
In my case, without him, I didn’t want to live.
I still regret it to this day.
Never forget your purpose in life.
If you have not discovered something to die for,
Then you are not truly living.
My time has ended…
I feel it coming…
Sweet relief.

Death. Some accept it, and some spend their whole life trying to avoid it; and yet they all have one thing in common... eventually, it always catches up with them.

“It is not death that a man should fear, but he should fear never beginning to live.”
– Marcus Aurelius

The Caged Bird


There once was a bird.
He was happy, because he was free.
Alas, it was not meant to be.
Now, he is trapped in his barred cage.
Wistfully looking through to the horizon, eyes brimming with rage.
Evermore now, the bird sleeps,
Dreaming of a better life, one where he was released.
Perhaps best unknown to him, the cage was never locked,
He just never tried to flee.
But just let him be,
Sleeping fitfully in his pain and misery.
Unknowing and ignorant, eternally.
Let him take safety in his prison, and find comfort in the wind…
For freedom is not freely given.

Therapy


I’m no longer who I used to be.
I don’t need you anymore.
I created you.
I don’t want you.
You’ve ruined my life.

“Locking yourself away won’t solve your problems.”
“I knew that”
“Then why?”
“I thought it was safer inside”
“Thought?”
“I wasn’t the only thing living in there”
Don’t act like you don’t know me.
Don’t treat me like I’m a stranger.
Without me, there is no you.
You were the one who invited me in,
I’ve been here this entire time.
